# Summary
Non-destructive ESL thermophysical measurements on CSS

# Abstract (Raw Extraction)
This paper discusses the current state of non-destructive research on the density, surface tension and viscosity of molten samples using the electrostatic levitation furnace (ELF) aboard the China Space Station (CSS). To address the challenges posed by the microgravity environment during the non-destructive testing process of containerless technology, we have developed several solutions. These include a temperature correction for infrared temper - ature measurement, a dual-camera position correction technique for density testing, a double electrode syn - chronous excitation, semi-frequency excitation and fine-sweeping approach for surface tension testing, and a signal demodulation method for viscosity testing. The uncertainties of the three thermophysical properties were 0.51 %, 3.10 %, and 9.78 %, respectively. This study highlights the feasibility and accuracy of measurements conducted in the ELF on the CSS, offering valuable insights into the behavior of molten materials under microgravity conditions. The findings from this study significantly contribute to our understanding of the thermophysical properties of molten materials.
