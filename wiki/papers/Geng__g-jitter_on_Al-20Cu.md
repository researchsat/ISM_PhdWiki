# Abstract (Raw Extraction)
. Understanding the solidification behaviour of high -performance light metal alloys is pivotal for tailoring microstructures and achieving the desired thermomechanical properties. A key element of this understanding is the experimental validation of sophisti cated numerical models. Real -time in-situ X- radiography has been used extensively to provide insight into solidification phenomena, including nucleation, primary growth, coarsening, solutal rejection and soft/hard grain impingement. Microgravity -based experiments used in conjunction with in -situ X-ray diagnostic equipment eliminate the influence of gravity, providing diffusion -limited solidification conditions and dramatically simplifying solidification dynamics. This work presents analysis of a p reviously performed microgravity solidification experiment carried out as part of the 58th ESA Parabolic Flight Campaign. During microgravity -based solidification, g -level fluctuations within the range of ±0.04 g were recorded via onboard accelerometers. To ascertain the potential influence of g -level fluctuations on equiaxed solidification the intergranular spacings between grain centres was measured as a function of time. Minor spatial fluctuations in the separation distances between grains was observed during solidification, however little-to-nodependence on measured g-level fluctuations was evident. Several equiaxed grains became trapped between the front/back crucible walls limiting their motion causing disruptions to growth of neighbouring grains. Add itionally, the mushy zone motion within the field of view (FOV) is likely to have disrupted the residual low-temperature gradient in the near-isothermal furnace, further influencing local solidification conditions. Follow -up work on simulating the solidification conditions presented is currently underway.
