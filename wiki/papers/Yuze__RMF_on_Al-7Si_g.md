# Summary
Rotating magnetic field modifies Al-7Si µg microstructure

# Abstract (Raw Extraction)
7 Directional solidifications of Al-7 wt.% Si alloy were carried out under microgravity on board 8 the International Space Station to investigate the impact of a rotating magnetic field (RMF) on 9 the solidified microstructure. It has been found that the RMF significantly influences the 10 solidified microstructure in the conditions corresponding to the lowest growth rate, whereas it 11 has negligible influence on the microstructure under the highest growth rate, indicating that the 12 RMF intensity and the forced liquid flow are too weak, compared to the solidification front 13 velocity, to impact the microstructure. For the lowest growth rate applied, the RMF application 14 results in a more uniform eutectic phase distribution and a smaller dendrite arm spacing. This 15 is ascribed to the RMF induced forced liquid flow that makes more uniform Si concentration in 16 the bulk liquid above the solid- liquid interface. Additionally, the RMF application possibly 17 modifies the columnar dendritic network by changing dendrite growth directions. This 18 observation can be attributed to the combined effect of the RMF induced forced liquid flow and 19 of the thermoelectric magnetic force on the dendrites resulting from the application of the RMF 20 as well. 21 22
