# ISS experiments (µg lab)

**Category:** platform

---

## Overview

The ISS hosts the most capable µg metal-solidification laboratory ever
flown: long-duration near-diffusive conditions for directional
solidification, plus ISS-EML and ESL containerless processing. Key
facilities: **SQF / LGF** furnaces in the MSL (NASA ESA Materials
Science Laboratory), **ISS-EML** (Columbus module), **MSG / MSRR**, and
the upcoming **XRMON-ISS** X-ray facility.

## Residual environment

Quasi-steady **g₀ ~ 10⁻⁶ g** plus [[wiki/flow/residual-gravity-gjitter|g-jitter]] from
crew and machinery. Marangoni flow is *not* suppressed, and spurious
grain formation has been documented ([[../Spurious grain formation due to Marangoni convection during directional solidification of alloys in u-g environment of International Space Station_SEYED.pdf|Seyed — Marangoni-driven spurious grains ISS]]).
