# Ti alloys (Ti-6Al-4V, TiAl)

**Category:** alloy  
**Platforms studied on:** [[wiki/platforms/ISS-experiments]], [[wiki/platforms/containerless-EML]]

---

## Overview

Titanium alloys — Ti-6Al-4V for aerospace structures, γ-TiAl for
turbines — are priority ISM materials. Their high melting point and
reactivity make **containerless** processing essential; hence the focus
on ISS-EML/ESL for Ti64 ([[../Precise Measurements of Thermophysical Properties of Liquid Ti-6AI-4V (Ti64) Alloy On Board the International Space Station_MOHR.pdf|Mohr — Ti-6Al-4V ISS-EML]]) and on centrifugal
Ti-46Al-8Nb CA-FVM modelling ([[../An Analysis of Solidification Experiments With a Ti-46AI-8Nb Alloy Under Centrifugal Conditions- Modelling of Flow-Solidification Interaction and Grain Structure Evolution Using a Cellular Automaton With Finite Volume Method_HUANG.pdf|Huang 2024 — Ti-46Al-8Nb CA-FVM centrifugal]]).

## Space relevance

Thermophysics from µg (density, viscosity, surface tension) feed
[[wiki/modelling/phase-field|phase field]] and [[wiki/methods/CFD-simulation|CFD simulation]] simulations for future orbital Ti AM
([[wiki/platforms/ISM-strategy|ISM strategy]], [[wiki/platforms/commercial-ISM-Varda|commercial ISM Varda]]). UHT-alloy Marangoni physics applies here
([[../Metastable Liquid Properties and Surface Flow Patterns of Ultrahigh Temperature Alloys Explored In Outer Space_WANG.pdf|Wang — UHT alloys & Marangoni in space]]).
