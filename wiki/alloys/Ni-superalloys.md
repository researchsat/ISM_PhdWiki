# Ni-based superalloys

**Category:** alloy  
**Key researchers:** [[wiki/authors/Christoph-Beckermann]]  
**Platforms studied on:** [[wiki/platforms/ISS-experiments]], [[wiki/platforms/containerless-EML]]

---

## Overview

Nickel-based superalloys (CMSX-4, René N5, IN718, IN939) dominate hot
sections of turbines. Their single-crystal DS processing is highly
sensitive to freckle defects ([[wiki/physics/freckle-segregation|freckle segregation]]) and to thermophysical
uncertainties — both of which ISS-EML and ESL experiments target.

## Space relevance

- ISS-EML gives viscosity, surface tension, and density of
  undercooled Ni-base melts — [[../Electromagnetic levitation containerless processing of metallic materials in microgravity- thermophysical properties_MOHR.pdf|Mohr — ISS-EML thermophysical properties]],
  [[../Electromagnetic levitation containerless processing of metallic materials in microgravity- rapid solidification_MATSON.pdf|Matson — ISS-EML rapid solidification]].
- UHT-alloy Marangoni patterns inform defect formation physics
  ([[../Metastable Liquid Properties and Surface Flow Patterns of Ultrahigh Temperature Alloys Explored In Outer Space_WANG.pdf|Wang — UHT alloys & Marangoni in space]], [[../Dynamic Localization Effect of Dendritic and Eutectic Growth Patterns Stimulated by Space Fluid Flow_LIAO.pdf|Liao 2024 — space flow on dendritic/eutectic growth]]).

## ISM angle

Ni superalloys are obvious targets for orbital casting and repair in
[[wiki/platforms/ISM-strategy|ISM strategy]]. Validated data at space temperatures (1500+ K) feed the
[[wiki/modelling/phase-field|phase field]] / [[wiki/modelling/CA-FE-models|CA FE models]] stack used for [[wiki/platforms/commercial-ISM-Varda|commercial ISM]].
