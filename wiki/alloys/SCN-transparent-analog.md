## Overview

**Succinonitrile (SCN)** and SCN-acetone / SCN-camphor mixtures are
transparent organic analogues whose cubic BCC solid phase mimics pure
metals. They enable **optical microscopy in real time** — impossible
for opaque metallic systems — making them the gold standard for testing
[[wiki/authors/Alain-Karma|dendritic-growth]] and eutectic theories.

## Space heritage

- **IDGE** (Glicksman, Shuttle era) — isothermal dendritic growth of
  pure SCN — set up classical tip-selection benchmarks.
- **[[wiki/platforms/DECLIC-DSI|DECLIC DSI]]-DSI** on ISS — directional solidification of
  transparent alloys: [[../Microgravity studies of solidification patterns in model transparent alloys onboard the International Space Station_AKAMATSU.pdf|Akamatsu — SCN transparent µg]],
  [[../In situ experiments in microgravity and phase-field simulations of th amellar-to-rod transition during eutectic growth_AKAMATSU.pdf|Akamatsu — lamellar-to-rod in µg]].

## Why it still matters

Validated µg SCN data constrain [[wiki/modelling/phase-field|phase field]] parameters (capillarity d0,
anisotropy ε₄) that are then transferred to metallic
[[wiki/alloys/Al-Cu|Al-Cu]], [[wiki/alloys/Al-Si|Al-Si]], and
[[wiki/alloys/Ni-superalloys|Ni-superalloy]] models.
