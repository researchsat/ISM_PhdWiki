## Overview

The **Columnar-to-Equiaxed Transition (CET)** is the switch from
directional columnar grains to randomly oriented equiaxed grains ahead
of the front. CET determines whether an ingot is anisotropic (columnar)
or isotropic (equiaxed) — a central lever for [[wiki/platforms/ISM-strategy|ISM]]
microstructure control. Nucleation of equiaxed grains arises from
heterogeneous substrates ([[wiki/methods/grain-refinement|inoculants]]) and from
fragmentation of existing columnar arms.

## Controlling variables

- Thermal gradient **G**, pulling velocity **V** — Hunt criterion.
- Inoculant density **N₀** and undercooling ΔTₙ (Greer / Quested).
- Convective velocity — ∆g, g-jitter, Marangoni
  ([[wiki/flow/residual-gravity-gjitter|residual gravity gjitter]], [[wiki/flow/marangoni-flow|marangoni flow]]).
- Alloy chemistry (Q, k) — [[wiki/alloys/Al-Cu|Al Cu]], [[wiki/alloys/Al-Si|Al Si]], [[wiki/alloys/Ni-superalloys|Ni superalloys]].
