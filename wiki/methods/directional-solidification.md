# Directional solidification (DS)

**Category:** method

---

## Overview

**Directional solidification (DS)** imposes a controlled thermal
gradient **G** and pulling velocity **V** to produce columnar or
CET microstructures. In space, DS is the backbone of the
[[wiki/platforms/CETSOL-MICAST-ICESOL|CETSOL/MICAST]] program and the [[wiki/platforms/DECLIC-DSI|DECLIC DSI]] DSI
facility — the Bridgman-type furnaces are space-qualified in the
MSL/SQF on ISS.

## Design variables

- **G, V** — Hunt CET criterion `V_crit ∝ G·f(N₀)`.
- **Grain-refiner density N₀** ([[wiki/methods/grain-refinement|grain refinement]]).
- **Convection strength** — Marangoni & buoyancy → DS on ISS suppresses
  buoyant part but retains Marangoni ([[../Spurious grain formation due to Marangoni convection during directional solidification of alloys in u-g environment of International Space Station_SEYED.pdf|Seyed — Marangoni-driven spurious grains ISS]]).
