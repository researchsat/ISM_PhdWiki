# Alain Karma

**Category:** author

---

## Overview

**Alain Karma** (Northeastern University) co-developed the
**thin-interface** phase-field method and the microsolvability
framework that cures the classical solvability problem of dendrite tip
selection. His 2001 Phys. Rev. E formulation is the de-facto standard
for quantitative alloy solidification simulations and underpins nearly
every paper in [[wiki/modelling/phase-field|phase field]].

## Why Karma matters here

- Quantitative [[wiki/modelling/phase-field|phase field]] for [[wiki/alloys/Al-Cu|Al-Cu]] underlies benchmark
  comparisons — [[../Benchmark Al-Cu Solidification Experiments in Microgravity and on Earth_THOMAS.pdf|Thomas 2023 — Al-Cu benchmark µg+1g]],
  [[../Nucleation and Growth Dynamics of Equiaxed Dendrites in Thin Metallic Al-Cu and Al-Ge Samples in Microgravity and on Earth_BECKER.pdf|Becker 2023 — Al-Cu / Al-Ge equiaxed µg]].
- Lamellar-to-rod eutectic work with Akamatsu draws on the same
  framework ([[../In situ experiments in microgravity and phase-field simulations of th amellar-to-rod transition during eutectic growth_AKAMATSU.pdf|Akamatsu — lamellar-to-rod in µg]]).
- ML pipelines trained on Karma-style PF synthetic data now segment
  experimental images — [[../Automatic Detection of Dendritic Microstructure Using Computer Vision Deep Learning Models Trained with Phase Field Simulations_VIARDIN.pdf|Viardin 2023 — CV/DL on phase-field dendrites]].
